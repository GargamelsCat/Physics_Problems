## 1. Vector Algebra

Given two vectors in 3D space:

$$
\vec{a} = [2, 1, -3]
$$

$$
\vec{b} = [4, -2, 1]
$$

We calculate:

a) Magnitude of each vector  
b) Dot product  
c) Cross product  
d) Angle between the vectors  

---

# a) Magnitude of Each Vector

The magnitude of a vector in 3D space is calculated using:

$$
|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}
$$

### Magnitude of vector a

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 1^2 + (-3)^2}
$$

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{4 + 1 + 9}
$$

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{14}
$$

---

### Magnitude of vector b

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{4^2 + (-2)^2 + 1^2}
$$

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{16 + 4 + 1}
$$

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{21}
$$

---

# b) Dot Product

The dot product formula is:

$$
\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + a_3b_3
$$

Substitute the vector components:

$$
\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (2)(4) + (1)(-2) + (-3)(1)
$$

$$
= 8 - 2 - 3
$$

$$
= 3
$$

---

# c) Cross Product

The cross product is calculated using the determinant:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b} =
\begin{vmatrix}
i & j & k \\
2 & 1 & -3 \\
4 & -2 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

Expand the determinant:

$$
= i(1 \cdot 1 - (-3)(-2))
- j(2 \cdot 1 - (-3)(4))
+ k(2(-2) - 1(4))
$$

Calculate each component:

$$
i(1 - 6) - j(2 + 12) + k(-4 - 4)
$$

$$
= -5i -14j -8k
$$

Final vector form:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = [-5,-14,-8]
$$

---

# d) Angle Between the Vectors

The angle between two vectors is calculated with:

$$
\cos\theta =
\frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}
{|\vec{a}| |\vec{b}|}
$$

Substitute known values:

$$
\cos\theta =
\frac{3}{\sqrt{14}\sqrt{21}}
$$

$$
\cos\theta =
\frac{3}{\sqrt{294}}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
\cos\theta \approx 0.175
$$

Angle:

$$
\theta = \cos^{-1}(0.175)
$$

$$
\theta \approx 79.9^\circ
$$

---

# Final Results

Magnitude of vector a:

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{14}
$$

Magnitude of vector b:

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{21}
$$

Dot product:

$$
\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 3
$$

Cross product:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = [-5,-14,-8]
$$

Angle between vectors:

$$
\theta \approx 79.9^\circ
$$
