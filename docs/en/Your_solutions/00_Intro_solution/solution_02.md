# Solving a System of Linear Equations

## Problem Statement
Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following system of equations:

1.  $2x + 3y = 12$
2.  $x - y = 1$

---

## Solution

We will use the **Substitution Method** to solve this system.

### 1. Isolate one variable
From equation (2), we can easily express $x$ in terms of $y$:

$$
x = y + 1
$$

### 2. Substitute into the other equation
Now, substitute this expression for $x$ into equation (1):

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

### 3. Solve for $y$
Expand the brackets and combine like terms:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

Subtract $2$ from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by $5$:

$$
y = 2
$$

### 4. Solve for $x$
Now that we have the value for $y$, substitute it back into our isolated equation for $x$:

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

## Final Answer
The solution to the system of equations is:

$$
x = 3, y = 2
$$



## Verification
To ensure the answer is correct, substitute the values back into the original equations:
* $2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$ (Correct)
* $3 - 2 = 1$ (Correct)
